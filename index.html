The Story

A City That Sings To Itself

MetroTech is an audio-visual fiction about three young women aboard an evacuation city-ship on the long voyage back to Earth — the world they fled as teenagers when the crisis came.

Between shifts on the broadcast relays, the rain grid, and the machine decks, they record music on aging analog equipment and transmit it on a dead channel — rebuilding the Earth they remember from tape hiss and teenage memory. Their broadcasts became the heartbeat of a homesick city.

The albums are those recovered transmissions.

The ship is called Meridian One. The crisis is not discussed. The voyage is long. The tapes are degrading — and that, too, is part of the record.

The Ensemble

Serin Hoshino

Broadcast Relay Operator Shift: Night Watch

The voice over the PA by day; seconded to comms watch on the command deck at night. Her night shifts among the dead channels are how MetroTech gets on the air. Extroverted, quick under pressure. Main vocals; plays guitars and keyboards.

Dani Okafor

Environmental Systems — Rain Grid Shift: Artificial Dawn

Runs the ship's artificial dawn and rain cycles, and works in the school library while she studies — the band's access to the media archive and its degrading materials from Earth. Grounding; first to show up when something goes wrong. She plays keyboards and synthesizers.

Sable Marsh

Systems & Maintenance Engineer Shift: Machine Decks

Machine rooms, transit decks, reactor levels. She hears the ship as an instrument, and scavenges and repairs the band's aging recording gear. Quiet, observational; keeper of the journal. Drums and drum machine programming.
